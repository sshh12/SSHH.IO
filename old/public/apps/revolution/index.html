By Shrivu Shankar

The Path to Revolution

The Cat-o-Meter

The Events that led to the Revolution
visualized with pictures of cats.

1

Proclamation of 1763

The Proclamation of 1763 drew a line across the Application Mountains to prevent colonial expansion westward. This was to prevent conflict with natives while Britain was still recovering from the French and Indian War.

This event is placed at 1 on the meter since it mildly increased tensions between them. This was because while the colonists were not allowed to settle, they knew the line was weakly enforced.

2

Sugar Act, 1764

The Sugar Act was created when Britain was in need of funds to pay past war debts and needed to reconsolidate its empire. While the act decreased the taxation of molasses, it strengthened the power of British Admiral Courts.

This event is placed at 2 on the meter since it mildly increased tensions between them. This was because the colonist viewed it as a way for Britain to profit from the colonies and the act took away trial by jury for colonial merchants.

4

Stamp Act, 1765

To raise funds after the French and Indian War, Britain passed the Stamp Act. With this, any time that was printed including newspapers, stamps, and books were taxed.

This event is placed at 4 on the meter since it moderately increased tensions between them. This was because colonists believed that their taxes should be passed by their colonial assemblies instead of Parliment, leading to petitions and boycotts.

3

Declaratory Act, 1766

After the repeal of the Stamp Act due to economic pressure, Parliment passed the Declaratory Act. This stated that Parliment had the right to pass acts and control the taxation in the colonies.

This event is placed at 3 on the meter since it mildly decreased tensions between them. This was because the colonist rejoiced over the removal of the Stamp Act and saw the Declaratory Act as meaningless.

6

Townshend Duties, 1767

When Charles Townshend came to power, he passed the Townshend Duties which aimed to regulate trade and not to increase revenue. This included the payment of colonial governors from Britain and the reinforcement of colonial regulatory agents.

This event is placed at 6 on the meter since it moderately increased tensions between them. This was because it took away colonists' control of their leadership and they responded with boycotts in large numbers, petitions, and newspaper attacks

11

Boston Massacre, 1770

After the passing of the Townshend Acts, colonists began to boycott and attack the British. To restore order troops were sent to Boston were a fight broke out and 5 colonists were killed.

This event is placed at 11 on the meter since it dramatically increased tensions between them. This was because it was used as propaganda and large protests broke out since it was the first time colonists were killed by the British soldiers for defiance.

12

Tea Act, 1773

Over time the British East India Company began to accumulate massive amounts of unsold tea. To sell it off they passed the Tea Act which was supposed to convince the colonists to purchase Company Tea.

This event is placed at 12 on the meter since it mildly increased tensions between them. This was because the colonists recognized the implications of the act, and dumped the tea out of the ships and the colonists were still being taxed without representation.

15

Quartering Act, 1774

With the passing of the Quartering Acts colonists were required to house British soldiers. The also had to provide them with food and other provisions.

This event is placed at 15 on the meter since it significantly increased tensions between them. This was because it forced colonists to maintain soldiers in their homes when all foreign enemies were removed.

20

Intolerable Acts, 1774

After the result of the Tea Act when colonists dumped royal tea into the harbor Britain needed a way to punish the colonists. They did this by passing the Intolerable Acts to demonstrate their control and to show that their actions will not go unnoticed.

This event is placed at 20 on the meter since it dramatically increased tensions between them. This was because it violated fundamental rights of colonists and constitutional liberties.

30

Lexington and Concord, 1775

To prevent a possible revolt British soldiers attempted to capture colonial leaders and war supplies. This led to the first military engagements of the revolutionary war.

This event is placed at 30 on the meter since it severely increased tensions between them. This was the first time the colonists battled the British and the first time blood was shed for the revolution.
